YouTube Summarizer

Just send a YouTube URL — AI summarizes the video and saves it to a spreadsheet automatically.

Watch on YouTube

Overview

Enter a YouTube video URL into the form, and the tool automatically fetches the transcript, has Claude (AI) summarize the content, and saves it as a new row in Google Sheets. No need to watch the video from start to finish — the key points are captured as text in seconds.

What Gets Saved

One-line summary

Grasp the whole video in a single sentence

5 key points

Main takeaways as a bulleted list

3 keywords

Tags describing the content

Timestamp

A record of when it was processed

Problem & Solution

Before

Every time I researched reference videos or webinars, I had to watch 10–20 minute videos start to finish and take notes by hand. The more videos, the more time it took, and finding them again later was a hassle.

After

Just paste the video URL and send. In seconds, a one-line summary, 5 key points, and keywords appear in a spreadsheet. You can grasp the content without watching the video, and over time it builds into a searchable research asset.

How It Works

01 Enter a YouTube URL into the form
02 Submit it
03 The summary, key points, and keywords are automatically appended to Google Sheets

Key Feature

The entire flow — fetching the transcript, summarizing with AI, and saving to a spreadsheet — is fully automated with no manual steps. The input can be swapped for email or a form, and the output for Notion or Airtable, depending on the workflow. Beyond YouTube summarization, think of this as one example of a general automation pattern: fetch information, process it with AI, and save it wherever you need it.
